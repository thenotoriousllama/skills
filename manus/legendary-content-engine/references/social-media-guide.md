# Social Media Guide

Platform-specific content creation for Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, and TikTok.
The core voice (intentional simplicity, layered emotion, controlled intensity,
human over hype) applies across all platforms. What changes is the adaptation.

## 1. Platform Adaptation Principles

### Adaptation Spectrum

| Platform | Length | Tone | Primary Pattern |
|----------|--------|------|-----------------|
| Facebook | Long-form (150-500 words) | Warm, narrative | Validation Through Specificity |
| LinkedIn | Medium (100-300 words) | Professional, insightful | Progressive Realization |
| Instagram | Short (50-150 words) | Sensory, intimate | Soft Reframe |
| TikTok | Micro (under 150 chars) | Sharp, immediate | Identity Disruption |

### Never Repurpose Directly

Content written for one platform must be adapted, not copy-pasted.
A Facebook post shortened for TikTok will feel wrong on both platforms.

## 2. Facebook

### Format
- Long-form narrative (150-500 words)
- Full emotional arc (all 4 beats)
- 1-2 images or 1 video per post
- Link in body text (not comments)

### Structure Template

```
[HOOK - 1-2 lines]
Pattern: Validation Through Specificity or Identity Disruption
"{{FB_HOOK}}"

[STORY/BODY - 3-8 short paragraphs]
Full four-beat arc. Short paragraphs. Line breaks for rhythm.
{{FB_BODY}}

[LANDING - 1-2 lines]
Pattern: Precision Statement or Soft Reframe
"{{FB_LANDING}}"

[CTA - 1 line]
Soft, conversational.
"{{FB_CTA}}"
```

### Facebook-Specific Rules
- The hook must work in the preview (first 2 lines visible before "See more")
- Use line breaks generously for mobile readability
- Do not use hashtags on Facebook (they reduce reach)
- Pair long-form posts with a single strong image

## 3. LinkedIn

### Format
- Medium-form thought leadership (100-300 words)
- Hook must land within first 210 characters (before "see more")
- Professional but not corporate
- 1 image or carousel

### Structure Template

```
[HOOK - 210 characters max]
Pattern: Identity Disruption or Precision Statement
"{{LI_HOOK}}"

[INSIGHT - 2-4 short paragraphs]
Pattern: Progressive Realization
{{LI_INSIGHT}}

[TAKEAWAY - 1-2 lines]
What the reader should think differently about.
"{{LI_TAKEAWAY}}"

[CTA - 1 line]
Professional, value-driven.
"{{LI_CTA}}"
```

### LinkedIn-Specific Rules
- First 210 characters are critical (the "see more" threshold)
- Use line breaks between every sentence for mobile readability
- 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end (not inline)
- Frame content as industry insight, not product promotion

## 4. Instagram

### Instagram Feed Post

```
[MICRO-STORY - 2-4 sentences]
Pattern: Soft Reframe or Validation Through Specificity
Sensory language. Intimate tone.
"{{IG_CAPTION}}"

[HASHTAGS - 3-5 relevant tags]
{{IG_HASHTAGS}}
```

### Instagram Reel Caption

```
[SHORT CAPTION - 1-2 sentences]
Paired with on-screen text in the video.
"{{IG_REEL_CAPTION}}"
```

### Instagram Stories
- Use polls, questions, and sliders for engagement
- Keep text to 1-2 short sentences per story frame
- Use brand colors and typography consistently

### Instagram-Specific Rules
- Captions should be 50-150 words for feed posts
- Use 3-5 targeted hashtags (not 30 generic ones)
- Alt text on every image for accessibility
- Carousel posts: each slide should stand alone but build a narrative

## 5. TikTok

### Format
- Micro-copy (under 150 characters for caption)
- 3-second visual hook imperative
- On-screen text carries the message; caption supports

### Structure Template

```
[CAPTION - under 150 chars]
Pattern: Identity Disruption
"{{TIKTOK_CAPTION}}"

[ON-SCREEN TEXT SEQUENCE]
Frame 1 (0-3s): "{{TIKTOK_HOOK_TEXT}}"
Frame 2 (3-7s): "{{TIKTOK_BODY_TEXT}}"
Frame 3 (7-end): "{{TIKTOK_LANDING_TEXT}}"
```

### TikTok-Specific Rules
- The first 3 seconds determine whether the viewer stays
- Caption is secondary to on-screen text and audio
- Use trending sounds when they align with brand voice
- Never use trending sounds that contradict brand tone
- 3-5 hashtags including 1-2 niche tags

## 7. AI Disclosure Requirements

### General Principle
Disclose AI-generated content transparently. This builds trust and
complies with emerging platform policies and regulations.

### Platform-Specific Disclosure
- **Instagram/Facebook (Meta)**: Use the "AI-generated" label when available.
  Include "Created with AI" in caption when label is not available.
- **TikTok**: Use the "AI-generated content" label. Include disclosure in caption.
- **LinkedIn**: Include "AI-assisted" or "Created with AI" in the post.

### What to Disclose
- AI-generated images (always)
- AI-generated video (always)
- AI-assisted copy (when substantially generated, not just edited)

### How to Disclose
Keep it natural and confident, not apologetic:
"Visual created with AI" or "AI-generated imagery" at the end of the caption.
