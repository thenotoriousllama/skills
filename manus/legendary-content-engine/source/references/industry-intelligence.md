### The Conversion Arc

Awareness to purchase follows a predictable emotional sequence:

1. **Awareness**: Reader encounters the brand (social, ad, referral)
2. **Recognition**: Reader feels seen by the content
3. **Understanding**: Reader grasps the methodology or value proposition
4. **Desire**: Reader wants the outcome
5. **Confidence**: Reader trusts the brand enough to act
6. **Action**: Reader purchases or signs up

Content should be mapped to specific stages. Do not try to accomplish
all stages in a single piece of content.

## 3. Platform Algorithm Intelligence

### Facebook/Instagram (Meta)

- Engagement-weighted: comments > shares > reactions > clicks
- Video content prioritized over static images
- Carousel posts receive extended exposure
- Posting consistency matters more than frequency
- Avoid engagement bait ("Tag a friend who...")

### LinkedIn

- Dwell time is a primary signal (longer reads = more distribution)
- Comments from connections boost reach significantly
- Native content outperforms external links
- First 210 characters determine click-through on "see more"

### TikTok

- Completion rate is the primary signal
- First 3 seconds determine whether the viewer stays
- Trending sounds boost initial distribution
- Consistent posting (daily or near-daily) builds momentum
- Niche hashtags outperform broad ones

### YouTube (Shorts and Long-Form)

- Click-through rate on thumbnail + title is primary
- Watch time and retention curve shape recommendations
- Shorts feed from the main Shorts shelf, not subscriptions
- Consistency in upload schedule improves channel authority

## 4. AI Disclosure and Regulatory Requirements

### Current Landscape (2025-2026)

AI-generated content disclosure is evolving rapidly. Stay current with:
- Platform-specific labeling requirements (Meta, TikTok, YouTube)
- FTC guidelines on AI-generated endorsements and testimonials
- EU AI Act transparency requirements for consumer-facing AI content

### Best Practices

- Always disclose AI-generated images and video
- Use platform-provided AI labels when available
- Include "Created with AI" or "AI-generated imagery" in captions
- For testimonials: never use AI to fabricate customer quotes
- For product claims: ensure all claims are substantiated regardless of AI involvement
