## 1. Email Voice Principles

### Tone
Every email should feel like a message from a thoughtful person, not a
marketing automation. The reader should feel respected, not targeted.

### Structure Rules
- One primary CTA per email
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Line breaks for rhythm and pacing
- CTA is an outcome, not a mechanic ("{{OUTCOME_CTA}}" not "Click here")

> Example: "See your colors" not "Click here to sign up"

### Formatting Rules
- Plain text preferred over heavy HTML templates
- Minimal images (1 max per email, if any)
- No banner headers that scream "marketing email"
- Mobile-first: test on phone before sending

## 2. Subject Line Methodology

### Principles
- Create curiosity or recognition, never urgency
- Under 50 characters for mobile preview
- No ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation
- No emoji unless it is core to brand voice

### Subject Line Patterns

**Curiosity**: "{{SUBJECT_CURIOSITY}}"
> Example: "What if it was never about taste?"

**Recognition**: "{{SUBJECT_RECOGNITION}}"
> Example: "You have felt this before"

**Specificity**: "{{SUBJECT_SPECIFICITY}}"
> Example: "The five things your closet is trying to tell you"

**Soft invitation**: "{{SUBJECT_INVITATION}}"
> Example: "We are almost ready"

### Subject Lines to Avoid
- "You will not believe..." (clickbait)
- "LAST CHANCE..." (urgency)
- "Exclusive offer just for you" (fake exclusivity)
- "Do not miss out" (FOMO)

## 3. Welcome Sequence

### Email 1: Welcome (Sent immediately)

```
Subject: "{{WELCOME_SUBJECT_1}}"
> Example: "This is just the beginning"

Body:
- Thank them without being effusive
- Set expectations for what they will receive
- Reinforce why they signed up (mirror their motivation)
- One soft CTA to explore {{BRAND_NAME}}

"{{WELCOME_BODY_1}}"
```

### Email 2: Value Introduction (Day 2)

```
Subject: "{{WELCOME_SUBJECT_2}}"
> Example: "Here is what most people get wrong"

Body:
- Pattern: Identity Disruption
- Introduce the core insight or methodology
- No selling. Pure value.

"{{WELCOME_BODY_2}}"
```

### Email 3: Deeper Understanding (Day 4)

```
Subject: "{{WELCOME_SUBJECT_3}}"

Body:
- Pattern: Progressive Realization
- Build on the insight from Email 2
- Show how {{BRAND_NAME}} addresses what they now understand

"{{WELCOME_BODY_3}}"
```

## 4. Nurture Sequence

### Purpose
Keep the relationship warm between purchase consideration points.
Every nurture email should give value, not ask for action.

### Nurture Email Template

```
Subject: "{{NURTURE_SUBJECT}}"

[OPENING]
Pattern: Validation Through Specificity
Name something the reader experienced recently.

[BODY]
Share an insight, tip, or perspective that helps them
regardless of whether they buy.

[CLOSE]
Soft, no-pressure. "{{NURTURE_CLOSE}}"
> Example: "When you are ready, we are here."
```

### Frequency
{{NURTURE_FREQUENCY}}
> Example: 1 email per week during nurture phase

## 5. Conversion Sequence

### Purpose
Move warm leads to purchase. Never pressure. Build inevitability.

### Conversion Email Template

```
Subject: "{{CONVERSION_SUBJECT}}"

[OPENING]
Pattern: Soft Reframe
Acknowledge where they are in their thinking.

[BODY]
Pattern: Progressive Realization
Build toward the natural conclusion that {{BRAND_NAME}} is the answer.

[SOCIAL PROOF]
One specific testimonial or data point.

[CTA]
"{{CONVERSION_CTA}}"
> Example: "See your colors."
```

## 6. Post-Purchase Sequence

### Email 1: Confirmation (Immediate)

```
Subject: "{{POST_PURCHASE_SUBJECT_1}}"
> Example: "You are in"

Body:
- Reinforce the value of their decision
- Set clear expectations for what happens next
- Practical next step (not another purchase)
```

### Email 2: First Value Delivery (Day 1-2)

```
Subject: "{{POST_PURCHASE_SUBJECT_2}}"

Body:
- Help them get the most from their purchase
- Specific, actionable guidance
- "{{POST_PURCHASE_TIP}}"
```

### Email 3: Check-In (Day 5-7)

```
Subject: "{{POST_PURCHASE_SUBJECT_3}}"

Body:
- Ask how it is going (genuinely)
- Offer support
- Share one additional insight
```

## 7. Upgrade Sequence

### Purpose
For products with tiers, move users from entry to premium.
Frame as natural progression, not upsell.

### Upgrade Email Template

```
Subject: "{{UPGRADE_SUBJECT}}"
> Example: "There is more to see"

[OPENING]
Acknowledge what they have already gained.

[BODY]
Pattern: Progressive Realization
Show what the next tier reveals that the current tier cannot.

[CTA]
"{{UPGRADE_CTA}}"
> Example: "See everything your colors can teach you."
```

### Upgrade Rules
- Never make the current tier feel incomplete
- Frame the upgrade as depth, not replacement
- Reference their specific usage or results when possible
- {{UPGRADE_TIMING}}

> Example: Send upgrade sequence starting at day 14 of entry-tier usage

## 8. Re-Engagement Sequence

### Purpose
Bring back users who have gone quiet. Lead with value, not guilt.

### Re-Engagement Email Template

```
Subject: "{{REENGAGEMENT_SUBJECT}}"
> Example: "We noticed something"

[OPENING]
Do not say "We miss you." Say something useful.

[BODY]
Share what has changed, improved, or been added since they last engaged.

[CTA]
"{{REENGAGEMENT_CTA}}"
> Example: "Take another look."
```

### Re-Engagement Rules
- Never guilt-trip
- Never use "We miss you" or "Where did you go?"
- Lead with new value, not emotional manipulation
- If 3 re-engagement emails get no response, reduce frequency rather than escalating

## 9. Transactional Emails

### Principles
Transactional emails (receipts, password resets, account updates) should
still carry the brand voice, just at lower intensity.

- Keep the brand tone but reduce emotional layering
- Be clear and functional first, on-brand second
- Include one subtle brand moment per transactional email

### Examples

**Receipt**: "{{TRANSACTIONAL_RECEIPT}}"
> Example: "Your Color Clarity Snapshot is ready. Here is your receipt."

**Password Reset**: "{{TRANSACTIONAL_PASSWORD}}"
> Example: "Let us get you back in. Here is your reset link."

**Account Update**: "{{TRANSACTIONAL_ACCOUNT}}"
> Example: "Updated. You are all set."
