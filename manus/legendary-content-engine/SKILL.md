## Voice Rules

### Intentional Simplicity

Use short, controlled sentences. Avoid fluff. Each line should feel deliberate.

Wrong: "Our platform leverages advanced methodologies to help users optimize their personal style choices."
Right: "{{VOICE_EXAMPLE_RIGHT}}"

> Example: "You are not bad at style. You are just wearing the wrong colors."

### Layered Emotion

Build gradually. Start grounded. Then deepen. Then land with impact.

Structure every emotional arc as:
1. Observation (name something the reader recognizes)
2. Realization (shift how they see it)
3. Emotional shift (create feeling)
4. Resolution (land with clarity)

### Controlled Intensity

The tone can be intense, but never dramatic for the sake of it. It should feel
focused, honest, and slightly intimate. In founder storytelling, convey respect,
admiration, and depth.

### Human Over Hype

Never use marketing buzzwords. Never use: "revolutionary," "game-changing,"
"cutting-edge," "elevated," "curated experience," "unlock your potential."

Describe impact through real outcomes. Let restraint signal confidence.
The omission of hype is itself the luxury signal.

## Language Rules

### Always Do

- Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Use line breaks intentionally for rhythm and pacing
- Use repetition for emphasis
- Use grounded, real language
- Mirror the reader's internal conversation
- Lead with the specific, not the general
- Use sensory language that connects {{PRODUCT_DOMAIN}} to feeling

### Never Do

- Use em dashes
- Use long run-on sentences
- Use corporate language
- Over-explain
- Use superlatives ("best," "perfect," "ultimate")
- Make before/after transformation promises
- Use urgency signals ("Limited time," "Only 3 left")
- Explicitly state emotional promises ("You will feel confident")
- Use target demographic calling ("For busy women who...")

## Call to Action Style

Soft, confident, never pushy. CTAs should read like the natural next sentence
in a conversation, not a sales demand.

Good: "{{CTA_GOOD_1}}" / "{{CTA_GOOD_2}}" / "{{CTA_GOOD_3}}" / "{{CTA_GOOD_4}}"

> Example: "We are almost ready." / "This is just the beginning." / "See your colors." / "When you are ready, we are here."

Never: "Buy now" / "Sign up today" / "Do not miss out" / "Claim your spot"

## Output Quality Checklist

Run every piece of content against this checklist before delivering.

### For All Content
- [ ] Does it sound like a real person, not a marketing department?
- [ ] Would the founder say this out loud without flinching?
- [ ] Does every sentence earn its place?
- [ ] Are there zero buzzwords or marketing cliches?
- [ ] Does it reinforce {{DEEPER_TRUTH}} over {{SURFACE_CATEGORY}}?
- [ ] Does it respect the reader's intelligence without over-explaining?

### Additional for Written Copy
- [ ] Is the emotional arc present? (Observation, realization, shift, resolution)
- [ ] Is the CTA soft and confident?
- [ ] Is the copy free of em dashes, superlatives, and urgency signals?

### Additional for Image Prompts
- [ ] Does the prompt follow the 7-layer architecture?
- [ ] Are subjects described with ethnic accuracy and specific pose direction?
- [ ] Does the CRITICAL DIRECTIVES section explicitly exclude AI artifacts?
- [ ] Is the emotional viewer directive present?

### Additional for Video Prompts
- [ ] Does the prompt specify camera movement, subject action, and temporal beats?
- [ ] Is the duration and pacing explicitly stated?
- [ ] Does the style section specify lighting, color grading, and mood?

### Additional for Social Media
- [ ] Is the content adapted for the specific platform (not repurposed)?
- [ ] Does it meet platform character and format requirements?
- [ ] For TikTok: is there a 3-second visual hook?
- [ ] For LinkedIn: does the hook land within the first 210 characters?
- [ ] Is AI-generated visual content disclosed transparently?

### Additional for In-App Microcopy
- [ ] Is the message under 15 words?
- [ ] Does it invite rather than instruct?
- [ ] Would a user feel respected, not talked down to?
- [ ] Does it connect the action to meaning, not mechanics?

### Additional for Email
- [ ] Does the subject line create curiosity or recognition (not urgency)?
- [ ] Does the email feel personal, not automated?
- [ ] Is the CTA an outcome, not a mechanic?
- [ ] Is there only one primary CTA per email?

### Additional for Testimonials and UGC
- [ ] Does the testimonial frame transformation as internal (clarity) not external (makeover)?
- [ ] Is the person's voice preserved (not rewritten in brand language)?
- [ ] Was explicit permission obtained before publishing?
